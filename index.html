Selection

Localization

The Brazilian Savannah encompasses the states: Amapá, Maranhão, Piauí, Rondônia, Distrito Federal,Goiás, Mato Grosso, Mato Grosso do Sul, Minas Gerais, São Paulo, Tocantins, Bahia. It is located in three of the largest hydrographic basins in South America, (Tocantins-Araguaia,São Francisco e Prata), which, in a way, favors its biodiversity.

Climate and Vegetation

The predominant climate in the cerrado is seasonal tropical characterized by a warm climate with rainy and dry periods. The vegetation is, for the most part, similar to that of savannah, with low, sparse trees, twisted trunks, thick leaves and long roots; grasses and shrubs.

Flora and Fauna of the Cerrado

The savannah and the ecosystems that compose it have a rich fauna and flora, being the habitat of many species of animals. This is also one of the reasons why this biome is one of the most affected by animal trafficking.

Cerrado Deforestation

In spite of presenting a rich great biodiversity, this biome has been suffering a lot with deforestation, mainly caused by agriculture. Today, the biome conserves only 20% of its total area, going through a great process of mischaracterization, that is, occupied by large cattle pastures and extensive plantations of soybeans, cotton, sugarcane, eucalyptus. In addition, much of the cerrado has already been destroyed by the unrestrained process of urbanization. Deforestation and illegal hunting, smuggling of species and fires, threaten the habitat of many species, thus leading to their extinction.

Onça Pintada

it is the biggest cat in the Americas and the third largest in the world, after tigers and lions. It is a predatory animal (at the top of the food chain), carnivore and vertebrate (has vertebrae). It belongs to the carnivorous order and the felidae family. Its scientific name is Panthera onca.

Jaguatirica

it is the third biggest mammal in the Americas, behind only the jaguar and the puma. Its most striking physical characteristic is the presence, in its coat, of yellow patches surrounded by black (in the horizontal position), on the legs, the patches are black in color and rounded in shape.

Lontra

it is a carnivorous, medium-sized and semi-aquatic animal that can be found throughout Latin America, except Chile. They can be viewed in environments such as forests, riparian forests, lakes, rivers and coastal regions associated with coastal lagoons.
